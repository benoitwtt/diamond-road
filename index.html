INTRODUCTION

The Diamond Road follows one of Brazil’s most historically significant corridors: the Estrada Real – Caminho dos Diamantes, a network of routes established in the 18th century to move diamonds and gold from the interior highlands of Minas Gerais toward the colonial centers of power.

This is not a smooth, paved cycling holiday.
It is a physically demanding, exploratory journey built around long climbs, mixed-surface roads, remote landscapes, and towns that still carry the rhythm of their past.

Over eleven stages, riders move slowly through geography shaped by extraction, trade, and isolation — experiencing the route at a human pace, through distance and effort rather than curated, pre-packaged moments.

The Diamond Road rewards patience, endurance, and curiosity.

THE EXPERIENCE

Each day follows a simple pattern: wake in a historic town, ride into the highlands, cross quiet valleys and ridgelines, and finish in another small community shaped by different layers of labor and lived history.
Some towns still carry the legacy of gold and diamond mining. Others are shaped by modern iron mining, small-scale agriculture, or a gradual shift toward eco-tourism.
You move through rolling terrain, dirt-road climbs, rural landscapes, and long stretches of quiet road with limited infrastructure.
Distances and terrain shift from day to day, but the experience is unified by steady effort, patient pacing, and openness to discovery.
The route follows an historic corridor. Its flow and cadence are shaped to feel like a continuous line through place and history rather than a series of highlights.

THE RIDING

The Diamond Road is a physically demanding mixed-surface route.
Expect:

Predominantly dirt, gravel, and compacted earth
Frequent steep climbs, often over 10%, with short sections occasionally exceeding 20%
A mix of steep climbs and rolling hills, with little extended flat terrain
Short paved connectors between communities, including occasional bone-rattling cobblestones

Technical riding skills are less important than endurance, pacing, and familiarity with sustained effort.
The reward is depth, not speed.

ACCOMMODATION & FOOD

Each night we stay in simple, comfortable local hotels, primarily in single-occupancy rooms, with shared rooms available for couples.
Lodging is selected for cleanliness, character, and alignment with the scale of each town rather than luxury or resort amenities.

Food is an essential part of the experience:

Regional Minas Gerais cuisine
Local restaurants and traditional recipes
Generous, satisfying meals after long days on the bike

Dinner is where the day settles and stories accumulate.

GROUP & SUPPORT

Maximum of 7 riders
Dedicated support vehicle for luggage transport, snacks, water, and rider support if required
Daily logistics coordination
Mechanical assistance within practical field limits
Emergency and evacuation planning per segment

Support is present but discreet.
The road remains the central character.

WHO THIS TOUR IS FOR

Diamond Road is for experienced riders who are comfortable with long, physically demanding days and variable road conditions.
It is best suited to riders who:

Enjoy sustained effort over multiple days
Are comfortable riding mixed surfaces
Value exploration, continuity, and place over speed or competition
Are adaptable and self-sufficient in remote environments

This is not a race, and it is not a luxury holiday.
It is a journey for riders who find meaning in effort, patience, and discovery.
